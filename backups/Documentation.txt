# Project: Sarcastic Signage

The layout for Sarcastic Signage establishes a clear, artistic brand identity that balances a handcrafted aesthetic with a direct, humorous tone.

### **Design and Brand Identity**

* **Header and Logo:** The site features the previously described watercolor gradient logo with its distinctive torn-paper bottom edge.
* **Typography:** The layout uses a clean, sans-serif font that mirrors the logo’s modern aesthetic.
* **Imagery:** The hero section showcases an authentic workshop environment, emphasizing that these are handmade products rather than mass-produced items.

### **Brand Identity and Tone**

* The "Anti-Inspirational" Mission: The brand explicitly rejects corporate tropes like "Live, Laugh, Love," focusing instead on relatable, unfiltered humor.
* Leadership: The business is a one-woman operation run by Patricia, who holds the title of "Chief Sarcasm Officer".
* Product Philosophy: Every item is handmade with "zero filter" and "delivered with attitude".

### **Business Operations**

* **Location:** The business is based in **Ottawa, Canada**.
* **Fulfillment:** All products are shipped; notably, local pickup is not an available option.
* **Ordering Methods:** Customers can place orders directly through the website, by phone, or in person at local events.
* **Support:** General inquiries and questions can be handled via email.

### **Project Log: Sarcastic Signage Web Development**

#### **1. Key Decisions & Strategic Direction**

* **Brand Authority:** Established Patricia as the "Chief Sarcasm Officer" to anchor the "unfiltered" and "relatable" brand voice.
* **Sales Model Priority:** Shifted the primary conversion goal from online browsing to **in-person sales** ("Catch Us in the Wild"), reflecting how the majority of products are actually sold.
* **Fulfillment Logic:** Corrected the initial "no pickup" assumption; the site must now explicitly support **local Ottawa pickup** and **Interac e-Transfer/Cash** payments.
* **Navigation Hierarchy:** Decided on a "Next Stop" top-level alert banner to create immediate urgency for the nearest market date.

#### **2. Context & Reasoning**

* **Anti-Corporate Aesthetic:** The design rejects "Live, Laugh, Love" tropes in favor of a handmade, "zero filter" aesthetic that mirrors the physical products.
* **Manual Order Flow:** Reasoning that since every item is handmade and unique, a "click-and-forget" e-commerce cart is less effective than a direct "screenshot and text" contact method.
* **Geographic Focus:** Restricted operations to **Ontario/Canada** to simplify logistics and reinforce the local maker identity.

#### **3. Key Constraints & Preferences**

* **Technical Stack:** **Self-hosted** environment using a single-file delivery of semantic **HTML, CSS, and Vanilla JavaScript**.
* **UI/UX Standards:** High priority on **scannability**, minimalist rhythm, and WCAG 2.2 accessibility.
* **Privacy/Content:** Explicitly **no photos of Patricia** are to be used in the "Meet the Maker" section.
* **Age Restrictions:** Must include a clear **18+ barrier** for the "Adults Only" magnetic canvas sign collection.

#### **4. Sample Content & Data**

* **Core Tagline:** "Handmade signs, totes & gifts with zero filter. Crafted by hand. Delivered with attitude."
* **The "International" Policy:** "No, we don't ship internationally. Yes, we've thought about it. No, we still won't."

## **Product Requirements & Specifications: Sarcastic Signage**

### **1. Brand Identity & Voice**

* **Core Persona:** "Chief Sarcasm Officer" (Patricia).
* **Tone:** Brutally honest, relatable, anti-inspirational, and "unfiltered."
* **Visual Language:** Minimalist UI/UX with high scannability.
* **Key Asset:** Watercolor gradient logo (Plum/Magenta/Orange/Green) with a "torn paper" bottom edge and white sans-serif typography.

### **2. Target Audience & Market**

* **Primary Demographic:** Adults (18+) seeking handmade gifts with "attitude."
* **Geographic Focus:** Ottawa/Ontario, Canada.
* **Sales Model:** Primarily in-person at events; secondary sales via phone/text/email for local pickup or domestic shipping.

### **3. Functional Requirements**

* **Event Management ("Catch Us in the Wild"):** A chronological list of upcoming markets with "Mark your calendar" functionality.
* **Hero Section:** High-impact display of the brand logo and a "Next Stop" event alert banner.
* **Catalog Display:** High-quality imagery of 200+ tote designs, wooden signs, and apparel.
* **Age-Gated Content:** A specific "Adults Only" section for $5 magnetic signs (18+ requirement).
* **Contact/Order Flow:** * No automated e-commerce checkout.
* Direct contact buttons for Call/Text (613-700-6677) and Email.
* Instructions for screenshotting items from photo albums.



### **4. Operational Specifications**

* **Shipping:** Canada-wide only; strictly no international shipping.
* **Pickup:** Local pickup available in Ottawa.
* **Payment:** Interac e-Transfer or Cash only.
* **Hosting:** Self-hosted (Non-Squarespace).

### **5. Technical Specifications**

* **Stack:** Semantic HTML5, CSS3 (Modern Flexbox/Grid), and Vanilla JavaScript.
* **Accessibility:** Adherence to WCAG 2.2 standards for contrast and scannability.
* **Responsive Design:** Mobile-first approach, prioritizing easy "tap-to-call" and "tap-to-text" functionality for users on the go at markets.

### **The Production Layout Strategy**

I have structured this to prioritize the **In-Person** sales model while keeping the "Chief Sarcasm Officer" persona front and center.

1. **The "Next Stop" Alert (Header):** A high-contrast, full-width banner at the very top. This is the first thing a visitor sees, creating immediate urgency for the next market.
2. **Hero Section:** Features the watercolor gradient logo with the "torn paper" edge effect. The primary CTA (Call to Action) will be "Find Us in the Wild," scrolling users directly to the event calendar.
3. **The "Meet the Maker" Block:** A clean, two-column section. Left side: A high-quality photo of Patricia in the workshop. Right side: The "No Live, Laugh, Love" manifesto.
4. **The "Catch Us in the Wild" Grid:** A structured table or card-based layout for the 2025 dates (Russel, Carleton, Kiwans, etc.), each with a functional "Mark your calendar" interaction.
5. **The "Unfiltered" Product Gallery:** A masonry-style grid showing the 200+ tote designs and the $5 "Adults Only" magnetic signs (with an 18+ content warning).
